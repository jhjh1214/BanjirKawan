# BanjirKawan — PowerPoint Build Sheet (10 slides, 5-minute online pitch)

Everything you need to build the deck, slide by slide. For each slide:
**ON-SLIDE COPY** (type this onto the slide — keep it tight), **BUILD** (layout /
diagram instructions), **SAY** (verbatim speaker script), plus timing and the
rubric points it banks. Build in 16:9. The deck is screen-shared over Zoom and *is*
the pitch — no live product demo (organiser's confirmed steer: "focus on your idea
and the business sides").

---

## Design system (apply to all slides)

- **Aspect:** 16:9 widescreen.
- **Fonts:** one clean sans throughout — Inter, Helvetica Neue, or Calibri.
  Headline 40–54pt bold; body 20–24pt; captions 14–16pt. Never smaller than 14pt.
- **Palette:**
  - Ink / text: `#0F172A` (near-black slate), secondary text `#475569`.
  - Accent (BanjirKawan blue): `#0284C7`, lighter `#0EA5E9`.
  - Background: white `#FFFFFF` or very light `#F8FAFC`. Keep it light — projects clean.
  - Threshold colours (for the map/diagram): normal `#16A34A` (green), alert
    `#EAB308` (yellow), warning `#F97316` (orange), danger `#DC2626` (red).
- **Style:** one idea per slide, lots of whitespace, no emojis, no clip-art. Numbers
  big and bold. Consistent 1–2 accent colours only.
- **Footer (optional, small):** "BanjirKawan · Climate Systems Hackathon 2026".

## SLIDE 3 — The system map  ← ANCHOR
**Timing 0:30–1:15 · Speaker A · Rubric: Systems Thinking (20) — the panel's core**

**ON-SLIDE COPY**
- Headline: **One broken link — the last metre**
- Diagram labels (see build).
- Callout on the broken arrow: **Highest leverage · Lowest cost · Our intervention point**

**BUILD** — this is the most important diagram in the deck.
- Left-to-right flow of boxes joined by arrows:
  `Rain` → `Rivers` → `JPS telemetry — ~200 stations, published thresholds` →
  `[ Agency dashboard ]`
  then a **thick RED dashed arrow labelled "THE LAST METRE"** pointing to →
  `Shop floor` → `Owner action` → `Damage / Claim` → `Loan / Recovery`.
- Colour the upstream boxes (left of the red arrow) in calm blue = "solved &
  expensive"; the downstream boxes in grey/red = "where the loss happens".
- Put a red circle/glow on the red dashed arrow. That's the whole thesis visually.
- **Iceberg inset** (small, bottom-right): 4 stacked layers labelled
  Event / Pattern / Structure / Mental model, waterline under "Event".

**SAY**
> "So we mapped the system. Rain feeds rivers; Malaysia has world-class telemetry —
> around two hundred live river stations with published danger thresholds. That
> data reaches an agency dashboard — and then it stops. Between the river gauge and
> the shop floor is a gap we call *the last metre*. Everything upstream — sensors,
> models, thresholds — is solved and expensive. Everything downstream — a flooded
> shop, a denied claim, a panic loan — flows from this one broken link. On the
> iceberg: the event is a flooded shop; the pattern is the same shops flooding every
> monsoon *despite* warnings; the structure is warnings that are river-centric with
> no shop-level translation and no evidence trail; the mental model is 'banjir is
> fate.' The brief's own question is *'what if floods double?'* — they will, and
> every extra flood widens exactly this gap. So the last metre is our intervention
> point: the highest-leverage, lowest-cost place in the whole system."

## SLIDE 5 — How it works (the design principle)
**Timing 1:45–2:05 · Speaker B · Rubric: Quality of Solution + Systems Thinking**

**ON-SLIDE COPY**
- Headline: **AI is never in the storm path**
- Left panel — **Dry day (AI thinks):** 5 photos → asset risk map → owner confirms
  → cached tiered playbooks
- Right panel — **Storm day (dumb + deterministic):** threshold → cache lookup → send
- Below/after — **After the flood:** 5 photos → claim report (evidence = river telemetry)

**BUILD**
- A vertical divider splitting "Dry day" (blue, calm) from "Storm day" (grey,
  minimal). A banner ribbon crosses the divider: **"AI is never in the storm path."**
- Small third strip beneath for the "after the flood → claim report" step.

**SAY**
> "BanjirKawan. The design principle answers the brief's own systems angle — *'the
> fix needs power, phones and roads too'* — so the AI is never in the storm path. On
> a dry day, AI vision turns five phone photos into a risk map of every asset — its
> height off the floor, its ringgit value — and the owner confirms it, so nothing is
> hallucinated. We pre-compute tiered action playbooks and cache them. When the
> flood comes, the alert path is dumb, deterministic code — threshold, cache lookup,
> send. And after the flood, five photos become an itemised claim report whose
> evidence is the river authority's own telemetry."
